LIBOR Market Model (BGM)

1997

The LMM/BGM Equation

The LIBOR Market Model directly models discrete forward LIBOR rates Lk(t) under their respective forward measures:

dLk(t) = Lk(t) [ μk(t) dt + σk(t) dWk(t) ]

Under the terminal forward measure QTN, the drift is determined by no-arbitrage:

μk(t) = ∑j=k+1N σk(t) ρkj σj(t) δj Lj(t) / (1 + δj Lj(t))
Key Insight

By modeling observable market rates (LIBOR forwards) directly, calibration to cap/floor volatilities is straightforward. Each forward rate is log-normal under its own forward measure, matching the Black caplet formula exactly.

Model Overview

The LMM models a set of forward rates L1, ..., LN resetting at times T1 < ... < TN.

  • Log-normal dynamics -- Each rate follows geometric Brownian motion under its own measure
  • Correlation structure -- Rates are correlated via dWi dWj = ρij dt
  • Monte Carlo simulation -- No closed form for swaptions; simulation is the standard approach
  • Market-consistent -- Caplet prices match Black formula by construction
  • Drift correction -- Under any single measure, drifts of other rates are adjusted for consistency

Why Correlation Matters

Caplet prices depend only on individual forward rate volatilities -- not correlations. But swaptions depend on the joint behavior of multiple rates. Higher correlation means rates move together, increasing swaption volatility. Lower correlation provides diversification, lowering swaption prices.
